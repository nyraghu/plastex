9 The product of two types

Just as function types are the Pi types of constant families, product types are the Sigma types of constant families.

Suppose \(X\) and \(Y\) are two types. We can then take the family in the description of Sigma types in Section 8 to be the constant family on \(X\) with value \(Y\). We denote the Sigma type of this family by \(X \times{} Y\): \begin{equation*}X \times{} Y \equiv\Sigma{}(x \mathrel{:}X).\, Y.\end{equation*} It is called the product of \(X\) and \(Y\). The elements of \(X \times{} Y\) are exactly the pairs for the family. These are objects of the form \begin{equation*}(x, y),\end{equation*} where \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\).

We have the projections \begin{equation*}\operatorname{pr}_{1}\mathrel{:}X \times{} Y \toX, \quad{}\operatorname{pr}_{2}\mathrel{:}X \times{} Y \toY.\end{equation*} They are defined by \begin{equation*}\operatorname{pr}_{1}(x, y) \equivx, \quad{}\operatorname{pr}_{2}(x, y) \equivy\end{equation*} for all \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\).

For any \(a \mathrel{:}X\) and \(b \mathrel{:}Y\), there are functions \begin{equation*}\sigma_{a} \mathrel{:}Y \toX \times{} Y, \quad{}\tau_{b} \mathrel{:}X \toX \times{} Y\end{equation*} defined by \begin{equation*}\sigma_{a}(y) \equiv(a, y), \quad{}\tau_{b}(x) \equiv(x, b)\end{equation*} for all \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\). As we vary \(a\) and \(b\), these functions give rise to functions \begin{equation*}\sigma\mathrel{:}X \toY \toX \times{} Y, \quad{}\tau\mathrel{:}Y \toX \toX \times{} Y.\end{equation*} These two latter functions are called the pairing functions.

The \(\eta\)-conversion for Sigma types implies that \begin{equation*}t \equiv(\operatorname{pr}_{1}(t), \operatorname{pr}_{2}(t))\end{equation*} for all \(t \mathrel{:}X \times{} Y\). This fact is called \(\eta\)-conversion for product types.
