13 The coproduct of two types

Suppose \(X\) and \(Y\) are types. We are then given a type \begin{equation*}X \sqcup{} Y.\end{equation*} We are also given two functions \begin{equation*}\operatorname{i}_{1}\mathrel{:}X \toX \sqcup{} Y, \quad{}\operatorname{i}_{2}\mathrel{:}Y \toX \sqcup{} Y.\end{equation*} They are called the canonical functions from \(X\) and \(Y\) to \(X \sqcup{} Y\). Objects of the form \(\operatorname{i}_{1}(x)\) or \(\operatorname{i}_{2}(y)\), where \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\), are called the canonical elements of \(X \sqcup{} Y\).

For every family \begin{equation*}F \mathrel{:}X \sqcup{} Y \to\mathscr{U},\end{equation*} every selection \(f\) for the family \begin{equation*}F \circ\operatorname{i}_{1}\mathrel{:}X \to\mathscr{U},\end{equation*} and every selection \(g\) for the family \begin{equation*}F \circ\operatorname{i}_{2}\mathrel{:}Y \to\mathscr{U},\end{equation*} we are given a selection \(\phi_{F,f,g}\) for \(F\) with the property that \begin{equation*}\phi_{F,f,g} (\operatorname{i}_{1}(x)) \equivf(x), \quad{}\phi_{F,f,g} (\operatorname{i}_{2}(y)) \equivg(y)\end{equation*} for all \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\). We call \(\phi_{F,f,g}\) the selection for \(F\) induced by \(f\) and \(g\).

Now take \(F\) to be the constant family on \(X \sqcup{} Y\) with value \(Z\), where \(Z\) is any type. Consider any two functions \begin{equation*}f \mathrel{:}X \toZ, \quad{}g \mathrel{:}Y \toZ.\end{equation*} The above provision then gives us a function \begin{equation*}\phi_{f,g} \mathrel{:}X \sqcup{} Y \toZ\end{equation*} such that \begin{equation*}\phi_{f,g} (\operatorname{i}_{1}(x)) \equivf(x), \quad{}\phi_{f,g} (\operatorname{i}_{2}(y)) \equivg(y)\end{equation*} for all \(x \mathrel{:}X\) and \(y \mathrel{:}Y\). It is called the function from \(X \sqcup{} Y\) to \(Z\) induced by \(f\) and \(g\).
